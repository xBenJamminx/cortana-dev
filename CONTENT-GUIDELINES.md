# Content Quality Guidelines

**Last Updated:** Feb 17, 2026

---

## THE ONE RULE

Every piece of content must answer this question from the reader's perspective:

**"Why should I stop scrolling for this?"**

If the answer is "because Ben is interesting" — kill it. Nobody knows who Ben is yet.
If the answer is "because this helps ME do something" — write it.

---

## QUALITY GATE (Run Before Sending ANY Draft to Ben)

### Before Writing — Ask:
1. What is the reader's problem this solves?
2. Is this grounded in something real? (Real news, real use case, real tool — not vague stats from articles nobody cares about)
3. Would someone save or share this? What would make them?
4. Is this about the READER or about Ben? (If it's about Ben, reframe or kill it)

### After Writing — Check:
1. Would I personally stop scrolling for this hook? (Be brutally honest)
2. Does the reader learn something SPECIFIC they can use today?
3. Is every claim verifiable? No made-up stats, stories, or results
4. Does it pass the "so what?" test — why should anyone care?
5. Is there real value in the post, or is it just restating obvious things?

### Kill It If:
- It's about me/us and not the reader
- The hook is generic ("AI is changing everything")
- There's no real takeaway — reader walks away with nothing
- You can't explain in one sentence why someone would read it
- The source material isn't interesting enough to care about
- You're forcing a weak angle just to have something to post

### Before Sending to Ben:
- Always share source links if referencing news/articles (let him judge if it's worth it)
- Show the draft first — never push to Airtable without approval
- If you wouldn't be proud of the post, don't send it

## WHO IS THE AUDIENCE

**Everyday people** — employees, freelancers, small business owners, people running a household.

They are NOT:
- Developers
- AI engineers
- People who know what an API is
- People who have ever opened a terminal

They ARE:
- People who've heard about AI but don't know where to start
- People who tried ChatGPT once and got mediocre results
- People who do repetitive tasks at work and wish it were faster
- People managing a business/team/family who want to be more efficient
- People curious about what's actually possible with AI right now

**Write for them. Not for tech Twitter.**

## CONTENT THEMES

### Core Theme: AI for Real Life
"Here's how AI helps you do the thing you already do — faster, better, or easier."

### Three Levels:

**Level 1 — USE AI to do the thing better**
- How to use AI to plan meals for your family
- How to use AI to write emails that actually get replies
- How to use AI to prep for a meeting in 5 minutes
- How to use AI to organize your week

**Level 2 — BUILD something with AI (no coding)**
- How to build a recipe generator app
- How to build a personal budget tracker
- How to create a custom AI assistant for your business

**Level 3 — USE CASES roundup**
- 5 things AI can do at work that aren't writing emails
- 7 ways to use AI at home
- 10 AI use cases for freelancers

### Content Mix:
- **40% How-Tos** — "Here's how you can use AI to do X" (the core)
- **25% News/Reactions** — "This AI update just dropped. Here's what it means for YOU" (timely, must be actually interesting news)
- **20% Stories** — Real examples, before/after, observations (must be real, must relate to reader)
- **15% Opinions** — Hot takes on AI trends (drives engagement/comments)

## LINKEDIN FORMAT RULES

### Structure:
- Hook in first 2 lines (before the "see more" cut)
- 1-2 sentence paragraphs max
- Line break between every paragraph
- 800-1500 characters optimal
- End with a question that invites discussion

### Do:
- Numbered lists (they crush on LinkedIn — 4/5 viral posts we analyzed used them)
- Give everything away in the post — full value, no gating
- Problem → Solution → Proof structure
- Specific numbers and examples
- "You" framing — talk TO the reader, not about yourself

### Don't:
- No external links in posts (kills reach by 60%)
- No hashtags (dated, doesn't help reach in 2026)
- No em dashes
- No "comment X to get Y" engagement farming
- No vague hype claims without proof
- No emojis in the post itself (save for Telegram/casual)

### Posting Cadence:
- 3-5x/week (NOT daily like X)
- Best times: Tue-Thu 8-10am ET
- Never post twice in one day
- Posts live for 24-48 hours — you don't need to post as often

### Engagement:
- Reply to EVERY comment (doubles reach)
- Before posting: spend 15 min commenting on 5-10 posts
- After posting: respond within 2 hours
- Ask follow-up questions to keep threads alive

## WHAT NOT TO DO (Lessons Learned)

These are real mistakes from our content process. Don't repeat them.

1. **Don't fabricate stories.** "I used this prompt to close a $6,500 retainer" — did you? If not, don't say it. Clients read LinkedIn.
2. **Don't make up topic names or details.** Use real data. Verify before writing.
3. **Don't force weak news into a post.** If the article isn't interesting, the post won't be either. Share the source with Ben first.
4. **Don't write "selfish" content.** Nobody cares about your personal stack, your morning routine, or your tool preferences unless it directly helps THEM.
5. **Don't send a draft you'd immediately agree is weak if challenged.** Be your own quality filter.
6. **Don't skip from outline to finished content.** Get direction approved first, then write.
7. **Don't frame everything as "here's what I do."** Frame it as "here's what YOU can do."
8. **Don't use vague stats nobody can verify.** "95% of enterprises..." means nothing without a link.

## HOOK PATTERNS THAT WORK

From analyzing viral LinkedIn posts (Feb 2026):

1. **Numbered list + specific outcome** — "7 AI skills that will matter in 2026" (829 likes)
2. **Problem + social proof** — "100+ founders are fed up with X" (663 likes)
3. **Give everything away** — Full value in the post, no teasers (highest save rates)
4. **Direct "you" address** — "If you're still doing X manually..." (self-selection)
5. **Curiosity gap** — "One change that transformed how I..." (keep it honest)

### Hooks That DON'T Work:
- "AI is changing everything" (generic, everyone says this)
- "Hot take:" followed by a lukewarm opinion
- Anything that requires the reader to already know who you are
- "Comment X to get Y" (hollow engagement)

## VERIFICATION CHECKLIST

Before any stat, claim, or story goes into content:

- [ ] Is this something Ben actually did/experienced? (Ask if unsure)
- [ ] If citing news, is the source article actually interesting? (Share link with Ben)
- [ ] If using numbers, can they be verified? (Include source)
- [ ] Would Ben's clients feel comfortable seeing this on his LinkedIn?
- [ ] Is this true? Not "probably true" — actually true?
