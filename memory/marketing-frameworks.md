## Key Frameworks We'll Use Most

### 1. Pricing: Three Axes
- **Packaging** — what's included at each tier
- **Pricing Metric** — what you charge for (per module, not per hour)
- **Price Point** — actual dollar amount (between next-best-alternative and perceived value)

### 2. Pricing: Good-Better-Best
- **Good** (Entry): AI Chief of Staff base — $1.5-2K/mo
- **Better** (Recommended): Base + 1 module — $2.5-3.5K/mo
- **Best** (Premium): Base + 2-3 modules + priority support — $4-5K/mo

### 3. Launch: ORB Framework
- **Owned**: Email list, blog, community (Discord/Whop)
- **Rented**: LinkedIn, X, YouTube (don't rely on these alone)
- **Borrowed**: Podcast interviews, influencer partnerships, Drew's network introductions

### 4. Launch: Five-Phase Approach
1. Internal Launch → test with Ben's own setup
2. Alpha → 1-2 trusted contacts from Drew's network
3. Beta → 5 clients, paid, with feedback loop
4. Early Access → word-of-mouth expansion
5. Full Launch → landing page, content engine, open for business

### 5. Copywriting: Page Structure
- **Above fold**: Headline (outcome without pain) + Subheadline + CTA
- **Social proof**: Logos, testimonials, numbers
- **Problem/Pain**: Show you understand
- **Solution/Benefits**: 3-5 key outcomes
- **How It Works**: 3-4 steps
- **Objection Handling**: FAQ, guarantees
- **Final CTA**: Recap + risk reversal

### 6. Social Content: Hook Formulas
- Curiosity: "I was wrong about [belief]"
- Story: "Last week, [unexpected thing] happened"
- Value: "How to [outcome] without [pain]:"
- Contrarian: "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"

### 7. Marketing Psychology: Top Models for Premium Services
- **Authority Bias** — expert endorsements, credentials
- **Scarcity** — limited client slots
- **Social Proof** — client results, testimonials
- **Loss Aversion** — what they lose by NOT having AI infrastructure
- **Anchoring** — show competitor prices first, then yours
- **Endowment Effect** — free audit/demo creates ownership feeling
- **IKEA Effect** — client customization increases perceived value
- **Mimetic Desire** — show desirable people using the service

### 8. Email: Welcome Sequence Structure
1. Welcome + deliver value (immediate)
2. Quick win (day 1-2)
3. Story/Why (day 3-4)
4. Social proof (day 5-6)
5. Overcome objection (day 7-8)
6. Feature highlight (day 9-11)
7. Conversion (day 12-14)

### 9. Referral Program: The Loop
Trigger Moment → Share Action → Convert Referred → Reward → Loop
- Double-sided rewards work best (both parties get something)
- Ask at peak moments (after aha moment, after milestone)
- Referred customers have 16-25% higher LTV

## Applied to OpenClaw Business

### Pricing Psychology to Use
- Round pricing ($2,500 setup, $1,500/mo) = premium signal
- Good-Better-Best with modules = natural upsell path
- Anchor with competitor pricing ($2,450-$9,750/mo range)
- "Less than a part-time EA" = mental accounting reframe
- Show total value, charge fraction = perceived deal

### Content Strategy for Lead Gen
- LinkedIn = primary (B2B, founders, execs)
- Content pillars: Build-in-public (30%), AI infrastructure insights (30%), Client outcomes (25%), Industry analysis (15%)
- 1 build per week → repurpose across platforms
- Every post → newsletter CTA → community funnel

### Launch Strategy for OpenClaw
- Phase 1: Drew's 3-5 warmest contacts (alpha clients)
- Phase 2: Refine based on feedback, build case studies
- Phase 3: Landing page + content engine + open enrollment
- Referral: Each client gets $500 credit for successful referral
