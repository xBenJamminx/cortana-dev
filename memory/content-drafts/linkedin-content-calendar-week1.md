# LinkedIn Content Calendar — Week 1

## Strategy Notes
- **Goal:** Establish Ben as a practical AI/automation authority on LinkedIn
- **Posting cadence:** 1x/day, Mon-Fri (weekends optional)
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu 8-10am ET for B2B audiences
- **Format mix:** Text-heavy posts (highest organic reach), occasional carousels
- **Hashtag strategy:** 3 per post. Mix: 1 broad (#AI), 1 niche (#AIAutomation), 1 specific (#BuildInPublic)
- **Algo notes (2026):** LinkedIn rewards dwell time > likes. Long-form text with line breaks performs best. NO external links in posts (kills reach by 60%). Engagement pods are dead. Reply to every comment.

## Day 2 (Tuesday) — VALUE POST / FRAMEWORK

**Format:** Text post
**Hook type:** Contrarian take + framework
**Source:** Prompt Engineering Guide

```
"Write me a blog post about AI" is the worst prompt you can write.

Here's why most people get garbage output from AI — and the fix:

You're missing 3 things:

1. PERSONA PRIMING
Don't just ask. Tell the AI WHO it is first.

"You are a senior content strategist who specializes in B2B SaaS. You've written for TechCrunch, First Round Review, and Lenny's Newsletter."

This alone changes everything.

2. CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT
Force the AI to think before it writes.

"Before writing, outline your reasoning: What's the core argument? What evidence supports it? What would make the reader stop scrolling?"

3. STRUCTURED CONSTRAINTS
Give it guardrails, not freedom.

"Write in short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max). Use specific examples, not generic advice. Tone: direct, opinionated, zero corporate speak."

The difference between a $0 prompt and a $500 prompt is specificity.

I've been engineering prompts for 2+ years. Tomorrow I'll share the exact prompt I use for client proposals that close at 80%+.

What's the one thing you struggle with most when prompting AI?

#PromptEngineering #AI #Productivity
```

**Why this works:** Teaches something actionable immediately. The "tomorrow I'll share" creates anticipation. Question at the end drives comments.

## Day 3 (Wednesday) — CASE STUDY / PROOF POST

**Format:** Text post
**Hook type:** Specific result + how I did it
**Source:** Business Ops Prompt Pack (Proposal prompt)

```
I used one AI prompt to write a client proposal that closed a $6,500/month retainer.

Here's the prompt (steal it):

"You are a senior business consultant preparing a proposal for [CLIENT TYPE].

Context:
- Client's problem: [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT]
- Our solution: [WHAT WE DO]
- Timeline: [EXPECTED TIMELINE]
- Budget range: [THEIR BUDGET]

Write a proposal that:
1. Opens with their problem (not our solution)
2. Shows we understand their specific situation with 2-3 observations
3. Proposes a phased approach (not a massive commitment)
4. Includes 3 pricing tiers (anchor high)
5. Ends with a specific next step (not 'let us know')

Tone: Confident but not salesy. Show expertise through specifics, not buzzwords.
Format: Clean headers, short paragraphs, easy to skim in 2 minutes."

The key insight: Start with THEIR problem, not your solution.

Most proposals lead with "About Us." Nobody cares about your company history when they're trying to solve a problem.

I've got 48 prompts like this in my vault. If this is useful, I'll share more.

#AIForBusiness #Proposals #ClientWork
```

**Why this works:** Specific dollar amount = credibility. Giving away the actual prompt = massive value. "I've got 48 more" = reason to follow.

## Day 5 (Friday) — PERSONAL STORY + LESSON

**Format:** Text post
**Hook type:** Story + business lesson
**Source:** Real experience / Competitor Analysis Guide

```
Last year I almost spent 3 months building a product nobody wanted.

I had the idea. I had the energy. I had the skills to build it.

What I didn't have: any idea what the competition looked like.

So before writing a single line of code, I spent 2 hours doing competitive analysis with AI.

What I found:
- 34 competitors I didn't know existed
- The "unique" feature I was excited about? 6 companies already had it
- BUT: a positioning angle nobody was using that was 10x more interesting

That 2-hour analysis saved me 3 months of building the wrong thing.

The framework:
1. Define your actual market (broader than you think)
2. Find EVERY competitor (not just the obvious ones)
3. Build a comparison matrix with AI
4. Map the positioning gaps
5. Find where nobody credible is competing

I turned this into a step-by-step guide with the exact prompts.

The best part? It works for any market, any industry, any size business.

Happy Friday. Go audit your competition this weekend.

#StartupLife #CompetitiveAnalysis #BuildInPublic
```

**Why this works:** Friday = story day on LinkedIn. The "almost wasted 3 months" hook is relatable to every founder/builder. Practical framework at the end.

## Week 1 Engagement Strategy

**Before posting each day:**
1. Spend 15 min commenting on 5-10 posts from target audience (investment firm partners, founders, AI creators)
2. Comments should be 2-3 sentences minimum, add genuine value
3. No "Great post!" — actually contribute something

**After posting:**
1. Reply to EVERY comment within 2 hours (doubles algorithmic reach)
2. Ask follow-up questions to keep threads alive
3. Thank people who share

**Connection requests:**
- After someone engages with your post, send a connection request with a note referencing their comment
- Target: 10-15 new connections/day from engaged users
