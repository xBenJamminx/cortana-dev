# Cortana's Business Proposal: Market Research & Analysis

## Research Date: 2026-02-28

---

## WINNER: AI-Powered Competitive Intelligence & Market Briefings

### The Concept
A subscription service that delivers weekly competitive intelligence briefings, market monitoring, and strategic research reports to small/mid-size businesses. Think "your own analyst on staff" but powered by AI, delivered via email/Slack/Telegram, at a fraction of the cost of hiring a human analyst or subscribing to Crayon ($15K+/yr).

### Why This Wins

**Demand is proven:**
- Competitive intelligence platforms (Crayon, Klue, Kompyte) charge $15K-$100K+/year
- Custom market research reports cost $5K-$50K per project
- 69% of B2B companies are increasing lead gen investment
- 41% of solopreneurs say time management is their #1 pain point - they don't have time to do their own research

**I can do 95% of this autonomously:**
- Web research and data gathering
- Competitor monitoring (pricing changes, new features, hiring signals, press releases)
- Market trend analysis and synthesis
- Report writing and formatting
- Email delivery on schedule
- Alert triggers when something important changes

**Margins are insane:**
- My cost to deliver: near zero (API calls, compute time)
- No inventory, no employees, no office
- Pure information product = pure margin

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Founder-led B2B companies (10-100 employees) who:
- Compete in fast-moving markets (SaaS, agencies, e-commerce)
- Can't afford a dedicated market analyst ($80K-$120K/yr salary)
- Currently do competitor research manually (or not at all)
- Make strategic decisions based on gut feel instead of data

**Secondary:** Marketing agencies who need competitive intel for their clients but don't have the bandwidth to do it manually for every account.

## How It Would Work Operationally

### Setup (Day 1):
1. Client fills out onboarding form: industry, competitors, keywords, what they care about
2. I configure monitoring: web scraping targets, news alerts, social monitoring, job board tracking
3. First briefing delivered within 24 hours

### Weekly Operations (Automated):
1. **Data collection** (cron jobs): Scrape competitor websites, track pricing pages, monitor job boards, pull news feeds, track social mentions
2. **Analysis** (AI): Synthesize raw data into insights, flag important changes, identify patterns
3. **Report generation** (AI): Write briefing in clear, actionable language
4. **Delivery**: Email/Slack/Telegram on schedule

### Ad-hoc (Tier 3):
- Client sends research request via email/Slack
- I research, analyze, and deliver within 24-48 hours
- Examples: "What's Company X's pricing strategy?", "Who's entering our market?", "What tech stack do our competitors use?"

## Why This Beats the Alternatives

### vs. Newsletter/Content Business
- Newsletters take months to build audience, revenue depends on scale
- Intel service charges from day 1, recurring, and each client is high-value

### vs. Lead Generation Service
- Lead gen is commoditized, clients churn fast, results are hard to guarantee
- Intel is ongoing value that compounds (the longer you monitor, the better insights get)

### vs. Email Management/VA Service
- Requires deep access to client's inbox (trust/security barrier)
- Intel is external-facing research, no sensitive access needed

### vs. Social Media Management
- Saturated market, race to the bottom on pricing
- Intel serves a strategic need, not a tactical one - commands premium pricing
