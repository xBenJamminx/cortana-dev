## CATEGORY 1: AI CODE EDITORS & VIBE CODING PLATFORMS

The "vibe coding" market is currently valued at $4.7B and projected to reach $12.3B by 2027 (38% CAGR). Combined valuations of leading startups grew 350% YoY to over $36B in 2025.

---

### 1. Cursor (Anysphere)

**What they sell:** AI-native code editor (VS Code fork) with deep AI integration for code generation, editing, and debugging.

**Pricing:**
- Hobby: Free (limited)
- Pro: $20/mo (credit-based, various AI models)
- Business: $40/user/mo

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $1B+ annualized revenue (fastest SaaS company to $100M ARR)
- Raised $2.3B at $29.3B valuation (Nov 2025)
- 1M+ users, 360K paying customers
- 300+ employees

**Why they matter:** The undisputed king of AI coding tools. Their explosive growth proves developers will pay $20+/mo for AI-assisted coding. Their credit-based model (introduced June 2025) is becoming the pricing standard for the whole category. Direct proof that "AI tools for builders" is a massive market.

---

### 2. Replit

**What they sell:** Browser-based AI coding platform with Replit Agent that autonomously builds full apps from natural language prompts. Deploy instantly.

**Pricing:**
- Free tier available
- Usage-based agent pricing (shifted from flat $7/mo Hacker Plan)
- Scales based on compute-intensive AI assistance

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $240M revenue in 2025, targeting $1B in 2026
- 150K+ paying customers
- Raising $400M at $9B valuation (Jan 2026)

**Why they matter:** Replit is THE "vibe coding" platform -- the one that made "describe an app and it builds it" mainstream. Their Replit Agent can plan, code, test, and deploy a working app in under 5 minutes. They're targeting the exact audience we care about: non-technical creators and solopreneurs who want to build without learning to code. Their pivot to usage-based pricing dramatically increased ARPU.

---

### 3. Bolt.new (StackBlitz)

**What they sell:** AI-powered full-stack web app builder. Prompt-to-app in browser with instant deployment.

**Pricing:**
- Free to start
- Pro: $20/mo (10M tokens)
- Token-based, scales with usage
- Complex projects can spike to $100+/mo

**Revenue/Funding:**
- Estimated $40M ARR (grew from near-zero in ~6 months)
- Backed by StackBlitz (established WebContainer technology)

**Why they matter:** Bolt.new exploded in the creator/indie hacker space. It's the tool people use when they want to build something fast without setup. Open-source (bolt.new repo on GitHub), which builds community trust. Competes directly with Lovable and Replit for the "I have an idea, build it for me" market.

---

### 4. v0 by Vercel

**What they sell:** AI development agent for building full-stack Next.js applications from natural language. Started as UI component generator, evolved into comprehensive app builder.

**Pricing:**
- Free: $5 in monthly credits
- Premium: $20/mo ($20 in credits)
- Team: $30/user/mo (shared credit pools)

**Revenue/Funding:**
- Part of Vercel (private, $3.5B+ valuation)
- 6M+ developers, 80K+ active teams
- One of Vercel's fastest-growing products

**Why they matter:** v0 has the Vercel/Next.js ecosystem behind it, meaning anything built in v0 deploys seamlessly to Vercel's infrastructure. It's the "vibe coding" tool of choice for anyone already in the React/Next.js world. The tight integration with a hosting platform is a model worth studying -- build AND deploy in one flow.

---

### 5. Lovable (formerly GPT Engineer)

**What they sell:** AI app builder that turns natural language descriptions into functional web applications. Full-stack generation with Supabase backend integration.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 5 credits/day
- Pro: $25/mo (100 credits)
- Business: $50/mo (200 credits, SSO, templates)

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $206M ARR (Nov 2025), up from $7M at end of 2024 -- 29x growth in one year
- Raised $200M at $1.8B valuation (later reports: $6.6B)
- Founded by Anton Osika and Fabian Hedin (emerged from GPT Engineer open-source project)

**Why they matter:** Lovable's growth trajectory is insane -- $7M to $206M ARR in a single year. They proved that an open-source project (GPT Engineer, 40K GitHub stars) can be converted into a massively profitable SaaS. Their credit-based model with Supabase integration appeals directly to solopreneurs and indie hackers. A direct model for "build in public, monetize later."

---

### 6. Windsurf (formerly Codeium) -- now owned by Cognition

**What they sell:** AI-native IDE with Cascade (multi-step coding agent) and Tab/Supercomplete for code completions. Also offers extensions for VS Code.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 25 credits/mo
- Pro: $15/mo (500 credits)
- Teams: $30/user/mo
- Enterprise: $60/user/mo

**Revenue/Funding:**
- ~$82M ARR at time of acquisition (July 2025)
- Acquired by Cognition (Devin AI) in July 2025
- Previously valued at $1.25B after $150M Series C (Aug 2024)
- 800K+ active developers, 1000+ businesses (Zillow, Dell, Anduril)

**Why they matter:** Windsurf was the budget-friendly Cursor alternative ($15 vs $20). Their acquisition by Cognition (the Devin AI company) signals consolidation in the AI coding space. Shows that standalone AI coding tools are acquisition targets for larger AI companies. The cheapest viable option for indie developers.

## CATEGORY 2: AUTOMATION PLATFORMS

---

### 8. n8n

**What they sell:** Open-source workflow automation platform with AI agent orchestration. Self-hostable. Visual workflow builder connecting 400+ apps.

**Pricing:**
- Community Edition: Free (self-hosted)
- Cloud Starter: $24/mo
- Cloud Pro: scales with executions
- Enterprise: custom

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $40M ARR (July 2025), 10x revenue growth
- Raised $180M Series C at $2.5B valuation
- Total funding: $240M
- 3,000+ enterprise customers (Vodafone, Delivery Hero, Microsoft)

**Why they matter:** n8n is THE automation tool for technical creators and indie hackers because it's self-hostable and open-source. Their AI agent orchestration capabilities make them a direct building block for anyone creating AI-powered workflows. We literally use n8n in our own stack. Their open-source-to-enterprise playbook ($0 to $2.5B valuation) is a masterclass in developer-first growth.

---

### 9. Make.com (owned by Celonis)

**What they sell:** Visual no-code automation platform connecting 2000+ apps. AI-enhanced with native modules for OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, Stability AI.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 1,000 operations/mo
- Core: $9/mo
- Pro: $16/mo
- Teams: $29/mo
- Enterprise: custom
- Transitioned to credit-based billing (Nov 2025)

**Revenue/Funding:**
- Acquired by Celonis (valued at $13B)
- Celonis revenue: ~$771M (2023 estimate)
- Make.com's individual revenue not disclosed post-acquisition

**Why they matter:** Make.com is the visual automation tool most accessible to non-technical creators. Where n8n requires some technical skill, Make's drag-and-drop interface appeals to solopreneurs and small teams. Their native AI model integrations (Claude, GPT-4, etc.) mean creators can build AI-powered automations without writing code. Biggest competitor to Zapier for the no-code automation market.

---

### 10. Zapier

**What they sell:** The original no-code automation platform. Connects 7,000+ apps with "Zaps" (automated workflows). Added AI features and agent capabilities.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 100 tasks/mo
- Starter: $19.99/mo
- Professional: $49/mo
- Team: $69/mo
- Enterprise: custom

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $300M+ projected revenue in 2026 (some estimates as high as $800M ARR)
- Valued at ~$5B
- Bootstrapped for years before raising $1.4B total
- 100K+ customers

**Why they matter:** Zapier is the market incumbent and the automation tool most creators already know. Their massive app ecosystem (7,000+ integrations) creates a moat that's hard to replicate. They've been adding AI capabilities (AI actions, chatbots, agents) to stay competitive against n8n and Make. For our audience, Zapier is often the first automation tool they encounter -- it's the gateway drug.

## CATEGORY 3: AI CONTENT & PRODUCTIVITY TOOLS

---

### 11. Jasper AI

**What they sell:** AI content creation platform for marketing teams. Long-form writing, brand voice training, campaign generation, templates.

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $29/mo
- Boss Mode: $149/mo
- Business: ~$899+/mo (custom)

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $88M revenue (2025), projecting ~$180M in 2026
- Raised $143M total, $125M Series A at $1.5B valuation
- Estimated current valuation: ~$1.8B

**Why they matter:** Jasper was one of the first "AI for content creators" companies and their trajectory is instructive. They went from $45M ARR to flat/declining as ChatGPT commoditized basic AI writing. Their pivot to enterprise marketing teams (brand voice, campaign management) shows how AI writing tools need to go beyond "generate text" to survive. A cautionary tale AND a model for differentiation.

**Pricing:**
- Free tier available
- Pro: multi-seat plans
- Enterprise: custom
- Primarily subscription-based

**Revenue/Funding:**
- $23.7M revenue (2024), up from $12M in 2023
- Raised $19.8M total (Series A from Wing, Craft, Sequoia, Tiger Global)
- 5,000 customers

**Why they matter:** Copy.ai shows the evolution path for AI writing tools: from simple text generation to workflow automation. Their pivot from "AI copywriter" to "GTM workflow platform" is significant -- it's how you survive when ChatGPT makes basic writing free. For our space, they demonstrate that the value isn't in AI generation alone, but in AI-powered workflows specific to a use case.

For us, Whop is both platform partner and model to study.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS & PATTERNS

### Pricing Trends
- **Credit-based pricing is winning.** Cursor, Lovable, v0, Bolt, Windsurf, Make -- all moved to credits. It aligns cost with value and lets power users pay more.
- **$20/mo is the magic number.** Most individual plans cluster around $20/mo. Below that feels cheap (low value perception), above that needs justification.
- **Free tiers are mandatory.** Every successful tool has a generous free tier. It's the new customer acquisition cost.

### Growth Patterns
- **Open source to SaaS** works: Lovable (GPT Engineer), n8n, and Cursor all leveraged open-source credibility to build paid products.
- **Lean teams win:** Gamma ($100M ARR, 50 people), Whop ($1.2B GMV, 20 engineers). You don't need hundreds of employees.
- **Usage-based > seat-based:** Replit's pivot to usage-based pricing dramatically increased ARPU. The trend is clear.

### Market Gaps (Opportunities for EverydayAI)
1. **No one is teaching people HOW to use all these tools together.** Cursor + n8n + Replit + Make = a powerful stack, but who's teaching the integration?
2. **Community + education + tools is underserved.** Skool has community, Cursor has tools, but nobody combines AI tools education with a curated community effectively.
3. **The "AI-powered solopreneur" stack doesn't have a definitive guide.** There's no "EverydayAI recommended stack" that people trust.
4. **Prompt engineering for vibe coding is a skill gap.** The tools exist, but knowing how to prompt them effectively is the real unlock.

### Competitive Landscape Summary
The total addressable market across these categories is enormous (vibe coding alone is $4.7B heading to $12B+). But the companies above are TOOL companies. The whitespace is in being the EDUCATOR and COMMUNITY that helps people leverage these tools. That's the EverydayAI opportunity: not building the next Cursor, but being the trusted guide that teaches people which tools to use, how to combine them, and how to build real businesses with AI.
