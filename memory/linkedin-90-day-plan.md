### Buyers (people who'd pay)
- Founder-led teams with 2-10 people doing too much manually

- AI-curious professionals at larger companies

**What they need from content:** Simple how-tos, "you can do this too" energy, no jargon.

## 4. CONTENT OS (4 Pillars + Weekly System)

### The 4 Pillars

**Pillar 1: TEACH (40%)**
Frameworks, how-tos, step-by-step breakdowns.
- "How to use AI to prep for any meeting in 5 minutes"
- "The 3-question framework for deciding what to automate"
- "How to set up an AI inbox triage (no coding)"

**Pillar 2: OPINION (25%)**
Hot takes, contrarian views, reactions to AI news.
- "Stop hiring VAs. Start building systems."
- "The most popular AI models aren't from OpenAI" (just posted this one!)
- "Why most AI automation projects fail"

**Pillar 3: BUILD (20%)**
Behind the scenes, documenting what you're actually doing.
- "How I run a consulting business with 3 people and AI"
- "What my morning looks like with an AI Chief of Staff"
- "I just onboarded a client's entire ops stack in 48 hours"

**Pillar 4: SELL (15%)**
Direct offers, case studies with clear outcomes, testimonials.
- "What a 30-day automation build looks like"
- "Client went from 40 hrs/week manual ops to 8"
- "We have 2 slots open for Q2 automation partnerships"

### Weekly System (Donnelly's Attention Engine)

**Thursday PM (Content Production Block):**
- 60 min: Idea sourcing (news, trends, client conversations, competitor content)
- 2 hrs: Batch write 3 LinkedIn posts for the following week
- Draft newsletter outline

**Friday PM (Polish + Schedule):**
- Finalize newsletter
- Review and polish LinkedIn drafts
- Queue posts for Mon/Wed/Fri

**Daily (15-20 min):**
- Before posting: Comment on 5-10 other people's posts (boosts your visibility)
- After posting: Reply to every comment within 2 hours
- This is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Engagement is 50% of the game.

**Weekly Review (Friday EOD, 20 min):**
- Which post performed best this week? Why?
- Which flopped? Why?
- What topics got the most comments?
- What should I do more of next week?

## 6. CONTENT FEEDBACK LOOP (Weekly Review Framework)

Every Friday, answer these 5 questions:

### Performance Check
1. **Top performer this week:** Which post got the most engagement? What made it work? (Hook? Topic? Format? Timing?)
2. **Bottom performer this week:** Which post flopped? What went wrong? (Weak hook? Wrong audience? Bad timing? Boring topic?)

### Pattern Recognition
3. **Topic heat map:** Which TOPICS are consistently getting comments? (Automation? AI news? Ops? Personal stories?)
4. **Format winners:** Which FORMATS work best? (Short text? Long text? Lists? Questions? Carousels?)

### Next Week Action
5. **Do more of / do less of:** Based on this week, what ONE thing should I do more of next week, and ONE thing should I stop doing?

### Monthly Metrics (Track in Airtable or spreadsheet)
- Total impressions this month
- Follower growth (net new)
- Total comments received
- Profile views
- Connection requests received
- Inbound DMs / leads
- Newsletter signups from LinkedIn

## 9. RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

### Always:
- End every post with a question (drives comments)
- Reply to EVERY comment (doubles reach)
- Spend 15-20 min engaging on others' posts BEFORE you post
- Use specific numbers and examples (not vague claims)
- Write for the reader, not about yourself
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- Line breaks between every paragraph

### Never:
- External links in the post body (60% reach penalty, put in first comment)
- Hashtags (dated, no reach benefit in 2026)
- Em dashes
- Emojis in the post itself
- "Comment X to get Y" engagement farming
- Vague stats you can't back up
- Post just to post (skip a day if nothing is good enough)
- Sell in Month 1 (pure value first)

### Format:
- 800-1500 characters optimal
- Hook in first 2 lines (before "see more" cutoff at 210 characters)
- Problem > Solution > Proof structure
- "You" framing, not "I" framing
