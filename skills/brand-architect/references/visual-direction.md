# Visual Direction Reference

## Color Psychology

### Primary Colors

**Red**
- **Evokes**: Energy, passion, urgency, excitement, power
- **Use for**: Calls to action, food/appetite, entertainment, sports
- **Caution**: Can feel aggressive; use sparingly as accent
- **Pairs well with**: Black (power), white (clean energy), gold (luxury)
- **Archetype fit**: Hero, Lover, Outlaw

**Blue**
- **Evokes**: Trust, stability, professionalism, calm, intelligence
- **Use for**: Finance, tech, healthcare, corporate
- **Range**: Light blue = approachable; Navy = authoritative; Electric blue = innovative
- **Pairs well with**: White (clean), orange (energy contrast), gray (professional)
- **Archetype fit**: Sage, Ruler, Caregiver

**Yellow**
- **Evokes**: Optimism, warmth, clarity, energy, happiness
- **Use for**: Children's brands, food, creativity, accessibility
- **Caution**: Can be hard to read; rarely works as primary
- **Pairs well with**: Black (high contrast), navy (sophisticated), gray (modern)
- **Archetype fit**: Innocent, Jester

### Secondary Colors

**Orange**
- **Evokes**: Friendliness, confidence, enthusiasm, creativity
- **Use for**: Food, fitness, youth brands, calls to action
- **Pairs well with**: Navy (trust + energy), white (fresh), dark gray (sophisticated)
- **Archetype fit**: Jester, Creator, Explorer

**Green**
- **Evokes**: Nature, growth, health, wealth, sustainability
- **Use for**: Environmental brands, health/wellness, finance, organic products
- **Range**: Bright = fresh/young; Forest = premium/established; Mint = modern/tech
- **Pairs well with**: White (clean), brown (natural), gold (wealth)
- **Archetype fit**: Caregiver, Explorer, Sage

**Purple**
- **Evokes**: Luxury, creativity, wisdom, spirituality, mystery
- **Use for**: Premium products, beauty, creative services, wellness
- **Range**: Deep purple = luxury; Violet = creative; Lavender = calming
- **Pairs well with**: Gold (luxury), white (elegant), black (dramatic)
- **Archetype fit**: Magician, Creator, Ruler

### Neutral & Accent Colors

**Black**
- **Evokes**: Sophistication, power, elegance, modernity, exclusivity
- **Use for**: Luxury, fashion, tech, premium positioning
- **Pairs well with**: White (classic), gold (luxury), any bold accent
- **Archetype fit**: Ruler, Outlaw, Magician

**White**
- **Evokes**: Purity, simplicity, cleanliness, minimalism
- **Use for**: Healthcare, tech, minimalist brands, backgrounds
- **Archetype fit**: Innocent, Sage

**Gray**
- **Evokes**: Neutrality, balance, professionalism, sophistication
- **Use for**: Tech, professional services, luxury, backgrounds
- **Range**: Light gray = modern; Charcoal = sophisticated
- **Archetype fit**: Sage, Ruler

**Gold/Metallic**
- **Evokes**: Luxury, success, premium quality, achievement
- **Use sparingly**: Accent only; overuse diminishes impact
- **Archetype fit**: Ruler, Magician, Lover

## Color Palette Construction

### Standard Palette Structure

1. **Primary color**: The dominant brand color (60% usage)
2. **Secondary color**: Complementary color for variety (30% usage)
3. **Accent color**: For highlights, CTAs, emphasis (10% usage)
4. **Neutral palette**: Grays, whites, blacks for backgrounds and text

### Palette Strategies

**Monochromatic**: Variations of single hue (sophisticated, cohesive)
- Example: Navy, sky blue, light blue
- Best for: Premium, minimalist, professional brands

**Complementary**: Colors opposite on wheel (dynamic, high contrast)
- Example: Blue + orange, purple + yellow
- Best for: Energetic, bold brands needing visual pop

**Analogous**: Adjacent colors on wheel (harmonious, natural)
- Example: Blue, teal, green
- Best for: Calming, cohesive, nature-oriented brands

**Triadic**: Three colors equally spaced on wheel (vibrant, balanced)
- Example: Red, yellow, blue (primary triad)
- Best for: Playful, dynamic brands

## Typography Direction

### Font Categories

**Serif**
- **Character**: Traditional, trustworthy, established, literary
- **Best for**: Luxury, editorial, finance, law, heritage brands
- **Examples**: Times New Roman, Garamond, Playfair Display, Merriweather

**Sans-Serif**
- **Character**: Modern, clean, accessible, tech-forward
- **Best for**: Tech, startups, healthcare, minimalist brands
- **Examples**: Helvetica, Inter, Open Sans, Montserrat

**Slab Serif**
- **Character**: Bold, confident, sturdy, approachable
- **Best for**: Brands needing presence without being too corporate
- **Examples**: Rockwell, Roboto Slab, Clarendon

**Display/Decorative**
- **Character**: Distinctive, expressive, unique
- **Use sparingly**: Headlines only; never for body copy
- **Best for**: Logo lockups, campaign headlines, creative brands

**Script/Handwritten**
- **Character**: Personal, elegant, creative, human
- **Use sparingly**: Accents, signatures, special occasions
- **Best for**: Luxury, artisanal, wedding/event, creative brands

### Pairing Principles

1. **Contrast is key**: Pair fonts with different characteristics (serif + sans-serif)
2. **Maintain hierarchy**: Display/heading font should be more expressive; body should be readable
3. **Limit to 2-3 fonts**: More creates visual chaos
4. **Consider weight range**: Ensure chosen fonts have multiple weights for flexibility

### Common Pairings by Brand Type

| Brand Type | Heading | Body |
|------------|---------|------|
| Luxury | Serif (Playfair) | Sans-serif (Light weight) |
| Tech Startup | Geometric sans (Montserrat) | Neutral sans (Inter) |
| Editorial | Serif (Merriweather) | Serif (same family) |
| Playful | Display/rounded | Friendly sans (Nunito) |
| Corporate | Sans-serif bold | Sans-serif regular |
| Artisanal | Script accent | Serif (warm) |

## Visual Mood Direction

### Photography Style Keywords

**By archetype tendency:**

- **Innocent/Caregiver**: Natural light, candid, warm tones, families, smiling faces
- **Explorer/Outlaw**: Outdoor, rugged, dynamic angles, natural environments, action
- **Sage/Creator**: Clean, well-composed, thoughtful subjects, detail shots
- **Hero/Ruler**: Powerful angles, dramatic lighting, achievement moments
- **Jester/Everyman**: Casual, relatable, humor, real people, everyday moments
- **Lover/Magician**: Intimate, sensual, atmospheric, dreamlike quality

### Describing Visual Direction

Use specific descriptors across these dimensions:

**Lighting**: Natural, studio, dramatic, soft, high-contrast, warm, cool
**Composition**: Minimal, busy, symmetrical, dynamic, close-up, wide
**Subject matter**: People, products, abstract, lifestyle, environmental
**Mood**: Energetic, calm, intimate, powerful, playful, serious
**Color treatment**: Saturated, muted, filtered, natural, black & white

### Example Visual Direction Statement

*"The visual identity uses natural, warm photography featuring real people in authentic moments. Compositions are clean but not sterile, with breathing room and natural light. The color palette appears in subtle accent elements rather than heavy filters. Overall mood: approachable confidence—professional without being corporate, warm without being casual."*
