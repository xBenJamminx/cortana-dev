# Viral Content Patterns

Formulas, hooks, and triggers for shareable content.

## Headline Formulas

### Number-Based (High Performance)
- **[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]** — "7 Ways to Double Your Email Open Rates"
- **[Number] [Noun] That Will [Benefit]** — "5 Tools That Will Cut Your Workflow in Half"
- **[Number] Mistakes [Audience] Make with [Topic]** — "9 Mistakes Founders Make with Their First Pitch Deck"
- **[Number] Lessons from [Authority/Experience]** — "12 Lessons from Scaling to $10M ARR"

### How-To (Utility-Driven)
- **How to [Achieve X] (Without [Pain Point])** — "How to Get Press Coverage (Without Hiring a PR Firm)"
- **How to [Achieve X] in [Timeframe]** — "How to Build an Email List in 30 Days"
- **How I [Achieved X] (And How You Can Too)** — "How I Got 100K Followers (And How You Can Too)"
- **The Complete Guide to [Topic]** — "The Complete Guide to Technical SEO"

### Curiosity Gap
- **Why [Counterintuitive Statement]** — "Why Your Best Employees Are About to Quit"
- **The [Adjective] Truth About [Topic]** — "The Uncomfortable Truth About Startup Culture"
- **What [Authority] Knows That You Don't** — "What Top Performers Know About Negotiation That You Don't"
- **I [Did Surprising Thing]. Here's What Happened.** — "I Deleted Social Media for 30 Days. Here's What Happened."

### Controversy/Hot Take
- **[Popular Belief] Is Wrong. Here's Why.** — "The 10x Engineer Myth Is Wrong. Here's Why."
- **Stop [Common Practice]** — "Stop Chasing Product-Market Fit"
- **Unpopular Opinion: [Statement]** — "Unpopular Opinion: Remote Work Is Overrated"
- **[Industry] Is Broken. Here's How to Fix It.** — "B2B Sales Is Broken. Here's How to Fix It."

### Social Proof
- **How [Company] [Achieved Result]** — "How Notion Built a $10B Company with No Sales Team"
- **What [Number] [People] Taught Me About [Topic]** — "What 500 Customer Interviews Taught Me About Pricing"
- **[Authority]'s Secret to [Outcome]** — "Warren Buffett's Secret to Long-Term Thinking"

### FOMO/Urgency
- **[Year]'s Ultimate [Topic] Playbook** — "2024's Ultimate LinkedIn Growth Playbook"
- **The [Topic] Trend Everyone's Missing** — "The AI Trend Everyone's Missing"
- **Before You [Action], Read This** — "Before You Quit Your Job, Read This"

## Emotional Triggers

### Curiosity
- Open loops (promise information, deliver later)
- Counterintuitive statements
- "Secret" or "hidden" language
- Questions that make readers think

### FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
- Time-sensitive framing
- Exclusive information positioning
- Trend/wave language
- Social proof of others benefiting

### Aspiration
- Paint the "after" picture
- Reference admired figures/companies
- Transformation narratives
- Success frameworks

### Fear/Pain
- Highlight consequences of inaction
- "Mistake" and "avoid" framing
- Risk-focused language
- Loss aversion triggers

### Validation
- "You're not alone" messaging
- Challenge conventional wisdom reader doubts
- Affirm reader's existing beliefs
- "Finally, someone said it" moments

### Anger/Frustration
- Call out bad actors/practices
- Challenge unfair systems
- "Why this needs to change" framing
- Righteous indignation

## Hook Templates

### The Scroll-Stopper (First Line)
```
[Counterintuitive statement that challenges assumptions]

Example: "The best marketing strategy is having no marketing strategy."
```

### The Story Hook
```
[Specific moment in time that creates intrigue]

Example: "It was 2 AM and I was about to hit send on an email that would change everything."
```

### The Data Hook
```
[Surprising statistic + implication]

Example: "73% of startups fail because of premature scaling. Here's how to know if you're next."
```

### The Confession Hook
```
[Admission that builds trust through vulnerability]

Example: "I've been lying to myself for 3 years about my business."
```

### The Question Hook
```
[Question reader can't help but answer mentally]

Example: "What would you do with an extra 10 hours per week?"
```

### The Contrarian Hook
```
[Statement opposing popular belief]

Example: "Hustle culture is a scam, and I have the data to prove it."
```

## Viral Content Structures

### The Thread/Carousel (Lists)
1. Hook (bold claim or curiosity gap)
2. Context (why this matters)
3. Items 1-N (one idea per unit, each standalone valuable)
4. Summary/CTA

### The Story Arc
1. Hook (moment of tension)
2. Background (minimal, just enough context)
3. Conflict/Challenge (what went wrong)
4. Turning point (insight or action)
5. Resolution (outcome + lesson)
6. Takeaway (actionable for reader)

### The Breakdown
1. Hook (why this example matters)
2. Context (what they did)
3. Analysis (why it worked)
4. Principles (transferable lessons)
5. Application (how reader can use this)

### The Framework
1. Hook (problem this solves)
2. Framework name + overview
3. Step 1 with example
4. Step 2 with example
5. Step N with example
6. Implementation advice

## Shareability Multipliers

**Make it about identity:** People share content that makes them look smart, helpful, or aligned with values they want to project.

**Make it useful:** Practical, actionable content gets saved and shared as a resource.

**Make it emotional:** Content that triggers strong emotions (awe, anger, anxiety, joy) spreads faster.

**Make it timely:** Connect to current events, trends, or conversations already happening.

**Make it novel:** First-to-market ideas, contrarian takes, or unexpected angles stand out.

**Make it simple:** Complex ideas packaged simply spread faster than simple ideas explained complexly.
