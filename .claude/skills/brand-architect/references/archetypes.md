# Brand Archetypes Reference

The 12 brand archetypes, derived from Carl Jung's work, provide a framework for establishing brand personality that resonates on a psychological level.

## Independence & Fulfillment Archetypes

### The Innocent
**Core desire**: To be happy and experience paradise
**Goal**: Safety, simplicity, happiness
**Fear**: Doing something wrong, being punished
**Strategy**: Do things right, be optimistic, see the good

**Voice characteristics**: Optimistic, honest, humble, wholesome, nostalgic
**Vocabulary**: Pure, simple, honest, natural, wholesome, fresh, classic
**Tone**: Warm, sincere, unpretentious

**Visual direction**: Clean, light, airy. Soft colors (white, pastels, sky blue). Simple, uncluttered compositions. Natural imagery.

**Brand examples**: Coca-Cola, Dove, Nintendo, Whole Foods, Innocent Drinks

---

### The Explorer
**Core desire**: Freedom to discover and experience the world
**Goal**: A more authentic, fulfilling life through discovery
**Fear**: Getting trapped, conformity, inner emptiness
**Strategy**: Seek out new experiences, escape the mundane

**Voice characteristics**: Adventurous, independent, ambitious, pioneering, restless
**Vocabulary**: Discover, journey, adventure, freedom, explore, authentic, trail
**Tone**: Inspiring, bold, independent

**Visual direction**: Rugged, natural landscapes. Earth tones with bold accents. Dynamic, expansive imagery. Outdoor and adventure photography.

**Brand examples**: Jeep, Patagonia, REI, The North Face, Red Bull

---

### The Sage
**Core desire**: To find truth and understanding
**Goal**: Use intelligence and analysis to understand the world
**Fear**: Being misled, ignorance, being duped
**Strategy**: Seek information and knowledge, be the expert

**Voice characteristics**: Knowledgeable, assured, guiding, articulate, thoughtful
**Vocabulary**: Truth, knowledge, wisdom, insight, understand, research, expertise
**Tone**: Authoritative but not condescending, educational, clear

**Visual direction**: Clean, intellectual aesthetic. Blues, grays, whites. Structured layouts. Data visualization, clean typography.

**Brand examples**: Google, BBC, The Economist, Harvard, TED

---

## Risk & Mastery Archetypes

### The Hero
**Core desire**: To prove worth through courageous action
**Goal**: Make the world better through mastery and determination
**Fear**: Weakness, vulnerability, being a coward
**Strategy**: Be strong, competent, and powerful

**Voice characteristics**: Confident, bold, inspiring, determined, honorable
**Vocabulary**: Triumph, achieve, courage, strength, victory, challenge, overcome
**Tone**: Motivational, empowering, direct

**Visual direction**: Bold, dynamic, high-contrast. Strong angles. Action photography. Primary colors, especially red and black.

**Brand examples**: Nike, FedEx, BMW, U.S. Army, Gatorade

---

### The Outlaw
**Core desire**: Revolution, breaking the rules
**Goal**: Overturn what isn't working, disrupt the status quo
**Fear**: Being powerless, ordinary, ineffectual
**Strategy**: Disrupt, shock, break conventions

**Voice characteristics**: Rebellious, provocative, bold, unapologetic, edgy
**Vocabulary**: Revolution, break, disrupt, defy, rebel, radical, unleash
**Tone**: Provocative, raw, authentic, counter-cultural

**Visual direction**: Dark, edgy, unconventional. Black with bold accent colors. Gritty textures, stark contrasts. Anti-establishment imagery.

**Brand examples**: Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel, Vans, Jack Daniel's

---

### The Magician
**Core desire**: Make dreams come true, transform reality
**Goal**: Create something special, make the impossible possible
**Fear**: Unintended negative consequences, losing magic
**Strategy**: Develop a vision and live by it, find transformative solutions

**Voice characteristics**: Visionary, inspiring, charismatic, imaginative, mystical
**Vocabulary**: Transform, magic, vision, imagine, create, dreams, possibilities
**Tone**: Inspiring, mystical, confident, transformative

**Visual direction**: Sophisticated, somewhat mysterious. Deep purples, blacks, metallics. Polished, premium feel. Unexpected visual moments.

**Brand examples**: Apple, Disney, Dyson, Tesla, Polaroid

---

## Belonging & Enjoyment Archetypes

### The Everyman
**Core desire**: Connection and belonging
**Goal**: To belong, fit in, be accepted
**Fear**: Standing out, being left out, hostility
**Strategy**: Develop ordinary solid virtues, be down-to-earth

**Voice characteristics**: Friendly, humble, authentic, relatable, unpretentious
**Vocabulary**: Together, real, honest, genuine, everyday, community, belong
**Tone**: Conversational, approachable, warm, inclusive

**Visual direction**: Warm, approachable, realistic. Earth tones, familiar settings. Real people (not models). Comfortable, lived-in aesthetic.

**Brand examples**: IKEA, Target, Budweiser, Home Depot, Gap

---

### The Lover
**Core desire**: Intimacy, connection, sensual pleasure
**Goal**: Being in relationship with people, experiences they love
**Fear**: Being alone, unwanted, unloved
**Strategy**: Become attractive physically and emotionally

**Voice characteristics**: Passionate, sensual, intimate, appreciative, devoted
**Vocabulary**: Love, desire, passion, beauty, indulge, devotion, pleasure
**Tone**: Warm, intimate, evocative, romantic

**Visual direction**: Sensual, luxurious, intimate. Rich reds, golds, warm tones. Close-up photography. Elegant, romantic imagery.

**Brand examples**: Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Häagen-Dazs, Godiva, Alfa Romeo

---

### The Jester
**Core desire**: To live in the moment with full enjoyment
**Goal**: Have a great time and lighten up the world
**Fear**: Being boring, boredom itself
**Strategy**: Play, make jokes, be funny

**Voice characteristics**: Playful, irreverent, fun, witty, spontaneous
**Vocabulary**: Fun, play, laugh, enjoy, party, crazy, hilarious
**Tone**: Lighthearted, humorous, energetic, cheeky

**Visual direction**: Bright, bold, playful. Saturated colors, especially primary. Unexpected combinations. Cartoon elements, humor in imagery.

**Brand examples**: M&M's, Old Spice, Dollar Shave Club, Skittles, GEICO

---

## Stability & Control Archetypes

### The Caregiver
**Core desire**: To protect and care for others
**Goal**: Help others, make a difference
**Fear**: Selfishness, ingratitude, instability
**Strategy**: Do things for others, be generous and compassionate

**Voice characteristics**: Caring, warm, reassuring, supportive, nurturing
**Vocabulary**: Care, support, protect, nurture, help, comfort, safe
**Tone**: Warm, empathetic, reassuring, generous

**Visual direction**: Warm, nurturing, soft. Soft blues, greens, warm neutrals. Family imagery, helping hands. Comforting, protective feeling.

**Brand examples**: Johnson & Johnson, TOMS, Campbell's, Volvo, UNICEF

---

### The Creator
**Core desire**: Create something of enduring value
**Goal**: Give form to a vision, express imagination
**Fear**: Mediocre vision or execution, inauthenticity
**Strategy**: Develop artistic control and skill, be innovative

**Voice characteristics**: Innovative, expressive, artistic, visionary, authentic
**Vocabulary**: Create, design, imagine, craft, innovate, express, vision
**Tone**: Inspiring, artistic, perfectionist, expressive

**Visual direction**: Creative, distinctive, artistic. Unique color combinations. Strong visual identity. Emphasis on craft and detail.

**Brand examples**: Adobe, Lego, Crayola, Pinterest, Apple (also Magician)

---

### The Ruler
**Core desire**: Control, power, success
**Goal**: Create a prosperous, successful organization or community
**Fear**: Chaos, being overthrown, losing control
**Strategy**: Exercise power, demonstrate leadership

**Voice characteristics**: Commanding, refined, articulate, confident, authoritative
**Vocabulary**: Power, success, premium, exclusive, superior, lead, prestigious
**Tone**: Authoritative, sophisticated, confident, formal

**Visual direction**: Luxurious, powerful, refined. Dark blues, blacks, golds. Clean, commanding layouts. Premium materials and finishes.

**Brand examples**: Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Microsoft, American Express, Louis Vuitton

---

## Selecting an Archetype

**Questions to guide selection:**
1. What emotional response should the brand evoke?
2. What fundamental human desire does the brand fulfill?
3. Who is the target audience, and which archetype would they identify with?
4. What archetype do competitors embody? (differentiation opportunity)
5. What is authentic to the brand's origin story and values?

**Primary vs. Secondary Archetypes:**
Most brands have one dominant archetype with possible secondary influence. Example: Apple is primarily Magician (transformation, making dreams reality) with Creator secondary (innovation, artistic expression).

**Archetype Blending Caution:**
Avoid blending archetypes from opposing quadrants (e.g., Outlaw + Caregiver) as they create contradictory messages. Adjacent archetypes blend more naturally.
